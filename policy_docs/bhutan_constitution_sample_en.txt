THE CONSTITUTION OF THE KINGDOM OF BHUTAN (SAMPLE SECTIONS)
==========================================================

ARTICLE 1: THE KINGDOM OF BHUTAN
1. Bhutan is a sovereign Kingdom and the Sovereign power belongs to the citizens of Bhutan.
2. The territory of Bhutan shall comprise of twenty Dzongkhags (districts).
3. The national flag and the national emblem of Bhutan shall be as specified in the First Schedule of this Constitution.
4. The National Language of Bhutan is Dzongkha.

ARTICLE 2: THE INSTITUTION OF KINGSHIP
1. The Druk Gyalpo (King of Bhutan) is the Head of State and the symbol of unity of the Kingdom and the people of Bhutan.
2. The Chhoe-sid Nyi (dual system of religion and politics) of Bhutan shall be unified in the person of the Druk Gyalpo, who, as a Buddhist, shall be the upholder of the Chhoe-sid.
3. The succession to the Throne of Bhutan shall be hereditary in the direct line of descendants of King Ugyen Wangchuck.

ARTICLE 7: FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS
1. A Bhutanese citizen shall have the right to life, liberty and security of person, and shall not be deprived of such rights except in accordance with the law.
2. A Bhutanese citizen shall have the right to freedom of speech, opinion and expression.
3. A Bhutanese citizen shall have the right to freedom of thought, conscience and religion.
4. A Bhutanese citizen shall have the right to practice any profession or to carry on any occupation, trade or business.

ARTICLE 10: THE PARLIAMENT
1. The Parliament of Bhutan shall consist of the Druk Gyalpo, the National Council, and the National Assembly.
2. The National Council shall consist of twenty-five members, comprising one elected member from each of the twenty Dzongkhags, and five eminent persons nominated by the Druk Gyalpo.
3. The National Assembly shall consist of a maximum of fifty-five members elected directly by the citizens of each constituency in Bhutan.
