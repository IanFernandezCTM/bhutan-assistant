BHUTAN CIVIL REGISTRY & IDENTITY SYSTEM GUIDELINES
==================================================

1. CITIZEN IDENTITY CARD (CID) ISSUANCE
Every Bhutanese citizen who reaches the age of 15 is eligible for a Citizenship Identity Card (CID).
- To apply for a CID:
  * Submit birth certificate or census registration record.
  * Submit recommendation letter from local Gup (head of village block).
  * Bring parents' CID copies for verification.
  * Apply online via the DCRC (Department of Civil Registration and Census) portal or in-person at the Dzongkhag head office.
- Card Validity:
  * A standard CID is valid for 10 years, after which it must be renewed for a fee of Nu. 100.
  * Replacement of lost or damaged CID cards incurs a replacement fee of Nu. 500.

2. BIRTH REGISTRATION POLICY
Every child born to a Bhutanese parent must be registered in the Civil Census Registry within 30 days of birth.
- If born in a hospital: The hospital issues a Birth Notification.
- If born at home: Gup verification is required.
- Registration document required: Copy of parents' CID cards, Marriage Certificate (if applicable), and Birth Notification.
- Penalty for late registration (after 30 days): Nu. 100.

3. MARRIAGE CERTIFICATE (MC) ACQUISITION
To obtain an official Marriage Certificate (MC) in Bhutan:
- Both parties must be 18 years or older.
- Submit application to the Royal Court of Justice (Dzongkhag Court).
- Required: Census clearance, CID copies, and presence of two adult witnesses (who must possess valid CIDs).
- Nu. 500 court registration fee.

4. DZONGKHA TRANSLATION HELPER (མི་རྩིས་)
- Census / Civil Registry: Mitsis (མི་རྩིས་)
- Identity Card: Lagkhyer (ལག་ཁྱེར་)
- Birth Date: Kyes-tshes (སྐྱེས་ཚེས་)
- Marriage: Nyen-jyor (གཉེན་སྦྱོར་)
