BHUTAN PERSONAL INCOME TAX (PIT) REGULATIONS
===========================================

1. WHO MUST FILE TAXES IN BHUTAN?
All Bhutanese citizens and residents whose annual net taxable income exceeds Nu. 300,000 are required to file Personal Income Tax (PIT).

2. TAX SLABS AND RATES
Personal Income Tax is calculated progressively based on annual income:
- Income up to Nu. 300,000: 0% (Exempt)
- Nu. 300,001 to Nu. 650,000: 10% of the amount exceeding Nu. 300,000
- Nu. 650,001 to Nu. 1,000,000: Nu. 35,000 + 15% of the amount exceeding Nu. 650,000
- Nu. 1,000,001 to Nu. 1,500,000: Nu. 87,500 + 20% of the amount exceeding Nu. 1,000,000
- Income exceeding Nu. 1,500,000: Nu. 187,500 + 25% of the amount exceeding Nu. 1,500,000
- Surcharge: A 10% surcharge is levied on the total tax liability if the net taxable income exceeds Nu. 1,000,000.

3. FILING DEADLINE & PENALTIES
- Filing Period: The tax filing window is open from January 1st to March 31st of every calendar year for the preceding tax year (income earned from Jan 1 - Dec 31).
- Late Filing Penalty: Nu. 100 per day of delay.
- Late Payment Interest: Interest at the rate of 24% per annum is charged on the outstanding tax amount from the date it becomes due.

4. PAYMENT METHODS
Taxes must be filed and paid online via the RAMIS (Revenue Administration Management Information System) portal. Mobile banking apps (mBoB, mPay) are supported.
